CIS PhD @ UPenn

Hi, I'm a third year PhD student in Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Rajeev Alur.

My research focuses on developing trustworthy AI models through neurosymbolic approaches, which combine neural learning with symbolic reasoning. I work on scaling these methods to complex, real-world scenarios and applying them in clinical settings. Currently, I am using these approaches for the early diagnosis of critical conditions, ensuring that predictions are grounded in clinical knowledge.

Previously, I received my Bachelor's degree from the University of Cambridge, where I worked on rep2rep with Mateja Jamnik.
